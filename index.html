What Makes This Plan Optimal

💪
Anterior/Posterior Gym Split
  • Every upper body muscle hit twice per week — proven superior for strength and hypertrophy vs once/week
  • Lower volume per session = less muscle damage = faster recovery between tri sessions
  • Mon AM: Anterior upper (chest, front delts, triceps) + easy run
  • Wed AM: Posterior upper (back, rear delts, biceps, rotator cuff) + easy run
  • Fri PM: Legs + Core — once weekly because bike/run already train your legs hard
  • Gym peaks in Off-Season, tapers through Base, becomes maintenance in Build, drops entirely in Peak
🏃
Proper Run Structure
  • 4 runs per week minimum — not just bolted-on gym day jogs
  • Dedicated tempo run session weekly from Base phase
  • Strides added in Base W5+ — neurological speed without fatigue cost
  • Long run progression: 60 min → 1:45 with proper Zone 2 pacing
  • Off-the-bike run (brick) treated as a separate quality session, not an afterthought
  • Race-pace half marathon effort bricks in Peak — not just "moderate"
🏊
Swim From Day One
  • CSS (Critical Swim Speed) work begins in Base W1 — not Build. Earlier speed stimulus = faster adaptation
  • Dryland technique work Jul–Aug 20 means pool day 1 is not starting from zero
  • Technique drills mandatory every session — poor mechanics at volume = injury and inefficiency
  • Open water practice added in Build when accessible
  • Race-pace 1,900m sets in Peak — 1.2mi at your actual race pace
  • Wetsuit practice before race day — 3–5 min free time if legal
🚴
Race-Specific Bike Intensity
  • 70.3 bike target: 75–85% FTP — NOT the conservative 68% of full IM
  • Sweet spot intervals (88–94% FTP) from Base W3 — builds the power you race at
  • VO2 intervals introduced in Build — raises the ceiling above race pace
  • Race-power bricks (75–82% FTP) in Build and Peak — legs learn what race day feels like
  • 2 full SIM DAYS in Peak: 56mi at race power → immediate half marathon
  • No junk miles — every ride has a purpose
📈
Matt Dixon Periodization
  • Strength PEAKS early (Off-Season) then systematically reduces — not a slow fade
  • Polarized training: 80% easy, 20% hard — no moderate junk zone in easy weeks
  • Recovery week every 4th week is mandatory — adaptation happens in rest, not stress
  • Brick workouts progress from easy → moderate → race pace over 6 months
  • Taper: 3 weeks, 40% volume drop each week, intensity maintained — arrives fresh and sharp
  • Race-week: 4 short sessions, nothing hard, just openers
⏰
Your Schedule Reality
  • Work 7am–4pm every day — all weekday sessions are 5–6:30am or 4:30–6:30pm
  • No pool until Aug 21 — dryland + bike + run + gym fills July/August
  • Weekends: Saturday is your big training day. Sunday is structured recovery.
  • Every 4th week volume drops 35–40% — these are not optional
  • Sunday = active recovery run or easy spin. Not full rest — keeps aerobic stimulus without fatigue
  • Off-Season Sunday = FULL REST. Base onward = easy active recovery Sunday

Gym Program

Anterior · Posterior · Upper Split

The split: Anterior (Mon) trains the front chain — chest, quads, front delts, biceps, forearms, abs. Posterior (Wed) trains the back chain — back, glutes/hamstrings, rear delts, triceps, calves. Upper (Fri) hits everything twice with a full upper body volume session. Every muscle gets hit 2×/week across the three days. 1–2 sets, 6–8–10 rep ranges, strength focus — this keeps sessions short enough to not interfere with swim/bike/run while still producing meaningful strength gains. Low volume per session also means minimal soreness going into the next day's training.

Why tibialis raises? The tibialis anterior (shin muscle) is chronically underloaded in cyclists and runners. Weak tibialis = shin splints, stress fractures, and poor foot strike mechanics. 3×20 tibialis raises twice a week is one of the highest-ROI injury prevention moves you can do. Use a tib bar, a seated resistance band, or simply the wall-seated raise — no equipment needed.

Gym Arc Across the Year:
Off-Season: MAX STRENGTH — 4–5 sets, heavy (3–6 reps on compounds). Your peak gym window.
Base W1–4: STRENGTH → POWER — 4 sets, heavier but explosive concentric. Faster up, controlled down.
Base W5–8: POWER → MAINTENANCE — 3 sets, 65–70% 1RM. Volume drops as tri volume rises.
Base W9–13: MAINTENANCE — 2 sets, 60% load. 20 min sessions. Just keeping what you built.
Build (Nov–Jan): UPPER DAY ONLY — Anterior Mon + Posterior Wed. Friday upper drops with legs in Feb.
Peak (Mar+): NO GYM. Tibialis raises and calf raises are ok — they're injury prevention, not fatigue.
Run on gym days: Monday and Wednesday start with a 20–25 min easy run AM before the PM gym session. Friday starts with a 20 min easy run before upper day. Upper body and the anterior/posterior splits do not significantly fatigue your run legs — these pair cleanly. Tibialis and calf work on these days directly supports run health.

Race Execution

Race Day — 70.3 Strategy

The 70.3 is a race, not a survival mission. You trained at race pace in practice. Now execute it. The bike is where the race is won or lost — go too hard and the run falls apart. Go correctly and you run the best half marathon of your life off the bike.
Fueling

Nutrition — Built for Training and Racing

The gut is a muscle. It needs training just like your legs. Start fueling correctly from Week 1 of Off-Season. Athletes who don't practice race nutrition in training discover gut problems on race day with no time to fix them.
